Two Sides One Choice

The player is in infancy stage
The player has two choices for each card for their actions
The player can also randomize their actions by pressing flip the coin
Thumbnail for Two Sides One Choice

Overview

A life simulation card game developed in 72 hours on itch.io where the player lives through 4 stages of life. Developed with a team of 5 people. I helped with parts of the programming and the design of the game including some of the cards. Won 5th place in the Ben Bonk game jam. Also created the trailer for the game. Most importantly, Two Sides One Choice marked my first game collaboration project (and first completed project) and the first team project started from University of Sussex Game Development Society (which I founded).

In the game, the player must keep their HP, bux and mood above zero at all times. If any of these reach zero, they will die which triggers an ending. The game features multiple endings depending on the player's actions and status. Status effects can be achieved by the player's actions, which affects choices that may appear and their basic stats.

Cards appear randomized but depending on the player's age and status effects.


Tools used

- Unity 2D.

- Adobe Premier to create the trailer.


What I learnt

- How to work in a team to create a game in 72 hours and how Game Jams work.

- The usage of GitHub for the first time, I learnt a lot about the issues that can happen such as merge conflicts.

- The use of .csv documents to store large amounts of data and how Unity can retrieve data from it, perfect for when you have +50 different cards the game needs to deal with.
